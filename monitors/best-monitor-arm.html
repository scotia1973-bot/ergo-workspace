Best Monitor Arm 2026: Top Gas Spring & Articulating Monitor Mounts

A good monitor arm is one of the most cost-effective ergonomic upgrades you can make. It frees up desk space, positions your screen at the perfect height and distance, and reduces neck and eye strain. We tested 14 monitor arms across single, dual, and heavy-duty categories to find the best monitor arm for your setup in 2026.

Why You Need a Monitor Arm

Your monitor's default stand is designed for the lowest common denominator — it's too low for most users, forcing you to slouch or tilt your head downward. An ergonomic monitor arm lets you position the screen so the top bezel is at or just below eye level, keeping your head in a neutral position.

According to ergonomic guidelines from the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), the top of your monitor should be at or slightly below eye level, and the screen should be about an arm's length away. A monitor arm makes this easy to achieve regardless of your desk height.

  • Range of motion — Height, tilt, swivel, and rotation

Monitor Arm Compatibility Guide

VESA Compatibility

Almost all modern monitors use the VESA standard mounting pattern — typically 75x75mm or 100x100mm. Check your monitor's manual or look for four screw holes on the back. Most monitor arms include both pattern types.

Weight Considerations

Always check the weight of your monitor (including the included stand) before purchasing an arm. A gas spring arm that's too weak will sag; one that's too strong will push upward. Many high-end arms have adjustable tension to accommodate different weights.

Desk Compatibility

Most monitor arms use a C-clamp that fits desks 0.5" to 3" thick. If you have a glass desk, you'll need a grommet mount or a special clamp. Standing desks with thinner desktops (like bamboo) may need reinforcement plates (usually included).

Build a Complete Ergonomic Setup

A monitor arm pairs perfectly with a standing desk — you can adjust your monitor height in seconds when transitioning between sitting and standing. Pair it with an ergonomic chair for optimal posture alignment.
